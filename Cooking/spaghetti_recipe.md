# Classic Spaghetti Bolognese Implementation

This guide outlines the systematic preparation of a high-performance Spaghetti Bolognese, focusing on efficient heat management and flavor optimization.

## 1. System Requirements (Ingredients)

Before beginning the build, ensure all dependencies are met. The following manifest lists the required components for a standard 4-person deployment.

### 1.1 Hardware and Ingredient Manifest
```yaml
ingredients:
  base:
    - spaghetti_pasta: 500g
    - ground_beef: 500g (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio)
  sauce_engine:
    - crushed_tomatoes: 800g
    - tomato_paste: 2 tbsp
    - yellow_onion: 1 medium (diced)
    - garlic_cloves: 3 (minced)
  flavor_modules:
    - olive_oil: 2 tbsp
    - dried_oregano: 1 tsp
    - salt: to taste
    - black_pepper: to taste
    - parmesan_cheese: optional (for garnish)
```

## 2. Environment Setup

### 2.1 Workspace Preparation
Ensure your cooking environment is clean and all tools are accessible. You will need a large stockpot for boiling pasta and a deep skillet or Dutch oven for the sauce preparation.

### 2.2 Heat Configuration
Set your stovetop to medium heat for the sauce components and high heat for the pasta water. Proper thermal management prevents the burning of aromatics (onions and garlic).

## 3. Implementation Steps

### 3.1 Executing the Bolognese Sauce
1. **Initialize Aromatics:** Heat the olive oil in your skillet. Add the diced onions and sauté until they reach a translucent state (approximately 5 minutes).
2. **Add Garlic:** Incorporate the minced garlic and sauté for an additional 60 seconds. Do not exceed this time to avoid bitter flavor profiles.
3. **Protein Integration:** Add the ground beef to the skillet. Increase the heat slightly and cook until browned. Ensure you break the meat into small, uniform granules. Drain excess fat if necessary.
4. **Sauce Compilation:** Stir in the tomato paste followed by the crushed tomatoes and oregano. 
5. **Simmer Loop:** Reduce the heat to low and let the sauce simmer for 20-30 minutes. This allows the flavors to merge and the sauce to thicken.

### 3.2 Pasta Compilation
1. Fill the stockpot with water and add a significant amount of salt (this is the only opportunity to season the pasta itself).
2. Bring to a rolling boil.
3. Add the spaghetti and cook according to the package parameters (usually 8-10 minutes) until 'al dente'.
4. Drain using a colander, but reserve 1/2 cup of the starchy pasta water to adjust sauce consistency if needed.

## 4. Final Deployment and Serving

### 4.1 Integration
Combine the drained pasta directly into the sauce skillet or plate the pasta and ladle the sauce over the top. For the best result, toss the pasta in the sauce for 1 minute over low heat to ensure complete coverage.

### 4.2 Output Verification
Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and cracked black pepper. Serve immediately while the thermal energy is at its peak.
