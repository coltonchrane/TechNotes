# Classic Spaghetti Bolognese Guide

This guide provides a structured technical approach to preparing a traditional spaghetti bolognese. It emphasizes the Maillard reaction, proper emulsification, and the development of complex flavor profiles through controlled thermal reduction.

[← Back to Index](../../index.md)

## 1. Specifications and Prerequisites

The success of this procedure relies on the quality of the raw materials and the precision of the *mise en place*.

### 1.1 Component List (BOM)

```yaml
Proteins:
  - 500g Ground Beef (80/20 lean-to-fat ratio for optimal lipid extraction)
Aromatics (Mirepoix):
  - 1 Large Onion (Allium cepa), 3mm dice
  - 2 Carrots (Daucus carota), 2mm mince
  - 2 Celery stalks (Apium graveolens), 2mm mince
  - 3 Cloves Garlic, microplaned or crushed
Liquids & Chemical Agents:
  - 800g Canned Crushed Tomatoes (San Marzano preferred)
  - 2 tbsp Tomato Paste (Triple concentrated)
  - 125ml Dry Red Wine (pH ~3.0-3.4, e.g., Chianti or Merlot)
  - 15ml Olive Oil (Extra Virgin)
Seasoning & Starch:
  - 500g Spaghetti (Durum wheat semolina)
  - 1 tsp Dried Oregano
  - Sea Salt and Freshly Cracked Black Pepper
  - Fresh Basil and Parmigiano-Reggiano for finishing
```

## 2. Execution Procedure

### 2.1 Thermal Softening of Aromatics (Sweating)

1. Heat olive oil in a heavy-bottomed Dutch oven or large pot over medium heat (approx. 150°C/300°F).
2. Introduce the onion, carrots, and celery.
3. Sauté for 8–10 minutes. The objective is to achieve translucency and cellular breakdown without inducing browning (caramelization).
4. Add the garlic; cook for 60 seconds until the volatile oils are released.

### 2.2 Lipid Rendering and Protein Browning

1. Adjust heat to medium-high to prepare for protein denaturation.
2. Incorporate the ground beef. Break the protein clusters into uniform aggregates using a flat-edged wooden spatula.
3. Sear the meat until it reaches a deep brown color, ensuring the Maillard reaction is achieved across all surfaces. 
4. **Optimization:** If excess lipid pooling occurs (exceeding 3 tbsp), drain the surplus fat to prevent the final sauce from becoming greasy.

### 2.3 Deglazing and Enzyme Breakdown

1. Add tomato paste to the center of the pot. Stir for 2 minutes until the paste darkens to a brick-red hue (caramelizing the sugars).
2. Pour in the red wine to deglaze. Use the spatula to physically detach the *fond* (browned bits) from the vessel floor.
3. Introduce crushed tomatoes and oregano. 
4. Reduce thermal input to low. Maintain a constant low simmer (approx. 85°C/185°F) for 45–90 minutes. This duration is required for the collagen in the meat to convert to gelatin, providing body and viscosity to the sauce.

## 3. Hydration and Final Assembly

### 3.1 Pasta Processing

1. Boil a high-volume water bath (minimum 4L) with a 1% salinity concentration.
2. Submerge spaghetti and cook until it reaches *al dente* (internal resistance remains).
3. **Critical Step:** Before draining, extract approximately 120ml (1/2 cup) of the starch-heavy cooking water.

### 3.2 Emulsification Phase

```bash
# Workflow for final sauce integration
STEP_1: Drain pasta and immediately transfer to the sauce vessel.
STEP_2: Introduce reserved pasta water in 30ml increments.
STEP_3: Agitate vigorously over low heat to emulsify the fats and starches.
STEP_4: Verify viscosity; the sauce should adhere to the pasta without pooling.
```

Serve at an internal temperature of 65°C–70°C. Garnish with freshly grated Parmesan and basil to provide aromatic top notes.
